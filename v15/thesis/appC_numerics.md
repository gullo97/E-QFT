# Appendix C — Numerical methods and reproducibility

## C.1 The reproducibility contract

1. **One number, one script.** Every number quoted in the thesis is printed by exactly one script, named at the chapter's *Validation* footer. No number enters the text from a notebook, a memory, or a previous run.
2. **Standalone scripts.** `numpy`/`scipy`/`matplotlib` only; no inter-script imports except the two shared modules below; every script runs from a bare clone via `python run_all.py [--fast|--all]`.
3. **Figures are build artifacts.** `thesis/figures/chNN_figM_*.png` are regenerated by the owning script (`--figures` where separated); the shared style is `scripts/style.mplstyle`. Hand-drawn schematics (concept figures) are flagged `(drawn)` in captions and exempt.
4. **Convergence is demonstrated, not asserted.** Any regulated or truncated quantity is reported with its convergence evidence: regulator scans (heat-kernel $t \to 0$ with Richardson extrapolation), basis-size scans, or lattice-size scans, as appropriate. A quoted error bar is the observed extrapolation residual, never a guess.
5. **Controls run with signals.** Wherever a theorem forces a zero (mirror spectra, diffeo sector, equal-wall quench), the zero is computed alongside the signal in the same run — the Ch. 11/25 discipline, institutionalized.
6. **Caches are committed, small, and disposable.** Heavy outputs land in `v15/data/` (`.npz`/`.json`); every cache is regenerable from its script; figures never read caches that the same run did not (or could not) produce.

## C.2 Shared module: the 1+1D Dirac bag (`bag1d`)

The workhorse of Part II (`ch08_bag_spectrum.py` exports it). The single-particle problem $\psi' = K(E)\psi$ with

$$K = \begin{pmatrix} -m_I & i(E + m_R) \\ i(E - m_R) & m_I\end{pmatrix}$$

is solved by **transfer matrix**: $\psi(L) = e^{KL}\psi(0)$, with $\psi(0)$ fixed to the wall ray $v_0(\theta_0)$ and an eigenvalue declared when $\psi(L)$ is parallel to $v_L(\theta_L)$ — the residual being the projection onto the orthogonal complement, $r(E) = \langle v_L^\perp, e^{KL}v_0\rangle$ (normalized). Root-finding: dense scan for local minima of $|r|$, then bounded scalar minimization to $10^{-13}$; deduplication by relative gap. Complete spectra below $E_{\max}$ are certified by the Weyl-count check (level density vs the asymptotic counting function). Validations: MIT spectrum vs $\tan(pL) = -p/m$ roots ($10^{-10}$); chiral master equation (12.7) at five angle pairs ($5\times10^{-8}$); the bulk-phase/$(\delta,\delta)$-wall equivalence ($8\times10^{-8}$).

**Spectral asymmetry.** $\eta(t) = \sum_k \operatorname{sgn}(E_k)e^{-t|E_k|}$ over *complete* spectra (both branches to the same $|E|$ ceiling — an asymmetric ceiling fakes asymmetry), evaluated on a ladder of $t$ values and Richardson-extrapolated to $t = 0$. Regulator-independence spot-checks (Gaussian, sharp-with-averaging) per Ch. 9.

**Vacuum charge density** (`ch11_wall_density.py`): $\rho_{\text{vac}}(x) = -\tfrac12\sum_k \operatorname{sgn}(E_k)|\psi_k(x)|^2 e^{-t|E_k|}$, same extrapolation pointwise; the regional integral cross-checks the Polarization Identity value.

## C.3 Shared module: ladder dynamics (`ladder`)

Part III's quantum simulations (`ch20`, `ch21`). Sparse tridiagonal Hamiltonians (up to $5200$ rungs); wavepacket evolution by Krylov `expm_multiply` with step-size control; observables $\langle n\rangle$, $\mathrm{Var}(n)$, $\alpha = \ln\langle n\rangle$, relational exponents by centered differences of $\alpha_a$ against the common time grid (clock-free by construction — any reparametrization cancels in $p_a$). Constraint-closed runs update the coupling $g(\alpha)$ self-consistently each step (240 updates over two e-folds in Ch. 21; halving the update interval moves results at $<10^{-3}$, reported). Fixed-point launches (Ch. 20) prepare Gaussian packets in $n$ with quasimomentum $\theta = \pm\pi/2$ imprinted as a phase ramp.

## C.4 Block-spectral lattice methods (Ch. 25)

Static metric perturbations depending on one coordinate block-diagonalize over exact transverse momenta: on $63^3$, the Laplace–Beltrami operator splits into $3969$ blocks of size $63$. Spectral (Fourier) derivatives on odd-sized grids (no Nyquist ambiguity); the metric enters through the covariant form $\partial_i(\sqrt g\,g^{ij}\partial_j)/\sqrt g$ discretized symmetrically to preserve self-adjointness. Unit tests gate the pipeline: flat-space spectrum exact to machine; uniform-shear spectrum to $3\times10^{-8}$ (exactly solvable by coordinate change). The covariant regulator $E(s) = \tfrac12\sum\omega_n e^{-s\omega_n}$ is evaluated per block and summed; the diffeomorphism control runs in every production sweep (contract C.1.5). Quartic fits $\Pi = \Pi_0 + \kappa q^2 + \beta q^4$ over $k = 1..5$ absorb $a_2$ contamination; fit covariances are propagated to the quoted $\kappa/C$ percentages.

## C.5 ODE and relaxation methods

Mixmaster (Ch. 24): the BKL-time ODE system with an adaptive high-order integrator; the Hamiltonian constraint evaluated along the trajectory as the quality certificate (initial residual $7\times10^{-18}$, drift $9\times10^{-13}$ — any run drifting beyond $10^{-10}$ is discarded by the script itself). Chain relaxation (Ch. 22): gradient descent on $E[\{L_i\}]$ with the short-range core; endpoint identities (totals, $\sqrt2 L_{\text{floor}}$) asserted programmatically. Newton lattice (Ch. 24): FFT Poisson solve on $128^3$; zero-mode handling documented in-script; fits exclude the small-$r$ discreteness region by a stated window.
