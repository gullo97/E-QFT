## 26.1 The unification

Translate Chapter 12 through the dictionary of Chapter 17. The pump's ingredients were: a box whose size changes (Part I's ladder traffic), a Dirac operator whose boundary data carry chiral angles (Part II's walls), and a level crossing whose spectral flow deposits one unit of charge. In metric language — which Part III has now earned the right to use literally, not metaphorically — the same sentence reads:

> **Charge is pumped when the spectral flow of the boundary Dirac operator crosses zero as the scale factor evolves** — anomaly inflow on a moving boundary of spacetime, with the wall chiral angles playing exactly the role the Goldstone–Wilczek angle plays in the descent equations.

Now read each Part as a face of that sentence:

- **Part I is the motion.** The arrow of expansion — the Expansion-Preference Theorem — is what makes the scale factor *evolve*, irreversibly, without an inflaton or tuned initial data. It is the engine of the pump (and, through Ch. 19, the ticking of the volume clock against which the propagating modes run).
- **Part II is the cargo.** The Spectral-Flow Master Theorem says geometry change converts to charge *only* through level crossings; the Wall-Mismatch Criterion says crossings exist only where boundaries carry CP structure. Matter content rides geometry change.
- **Part III is the arena.** The boxes whose expansion does the pumping *are* the metric — exactly (Dictionary Theorem), not analogically — and their collective dynamics is Einstein-like with measured coefficients: the boundary on which the anomaly flows is a boundary *of spacetime itself*.

One object underlies all three: **matter on a dynamical, discrete geometry whose boundaries carry the CP physics.** The expansion arrow, the baryon excess, and gravity are its three projections. The ECPT conjecture of Ch. 7 — the proposal that the arrows are *locked*, not merely coexistent — is the statement that the projections share a sign; it remains a conjecture, and the structure stands without it.

## 26.3 What would falsify it

A defensible theory must volunteer its own executioners. In ascending order of finality: the **conformal magnitude** failing to converge to $+C(s)$ as $qs \to 0$ (would break the one-loop EH identification); the **dynamical response** $\Pi(\omega, q)$ yielding a wave speed $\ne 1$ or an induced mass (would kill the graviton claim); the **3+1D spherical chiral bag** showing zero crossing density in all channels (would kill the baryogenesis mechanism's uplift); the **microscopic constraint test** — conservation of (induced geometry energy + matter energy) under ladder dynamics — failing (would demote Part III's cosmology to phenomenology); and the **boundary-GIM computation** pinning $\varepsilon_{CP}$ at its crushed value while $\mathcal P_\times > 0$ (would put $\eta_B$ nine orders below observation, falsifying the framework's baryogenesis even with the mechanism intact). Each is a posed calculation, not a vibe — they are items 1–6 of Ch. 27.

## 26.4 What is *not* claimed

For the record, and for the defense. The thesis does **not** claim: a solution to the cosmological-constant problem (the $\Lambda^4$/$V/s^4$ term stands untouched, every appearance flagged); a derivation of the Lorentzian signature and lapse/shift sector (the negative compression sign is the seed, Ch. 27 item 5 the plan); a derived value of $\eta_B$ (a structure plus a budget, with the deciding calculation named); a proof of the ECPT conjecture (an organizing principle whose failure costs the sign-lock, not the results); or uniqueness of the microscopic postulates (sector arena and nearest-neighbour rule are stated as postulates and used as such). What it does claim is narrower and, we contend, defensible line by line: *a single discrete structure, with two kinematic postulates and one imported constraint, from which the arrow of expansion, anomaly-protected charge pumping, and the leading structures of general relativity — orbits, cosmologies, Newton, BKL, and the graviton's measured kinetic term — all follow, each step either proved, computed to stated precision, or labeled.*
